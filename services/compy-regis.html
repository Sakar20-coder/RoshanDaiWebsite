Company Registration in Nepal

Company registration is the legal process through which a business receives official recognition from the Government of Nepal. Once registered, a company can legally operate, open bank accounts, enter contracts, and comply with tax obligations.

Overview

Company registration in Nepal is governed by the Companies Act 2063 and regulated by the Office of the Company Registrar (OCR). With the introduction of digital services, entrepreneurs can now complete most of the registration process online.

Types of Companies in Nepal

Private Limited Company

The most common form of business structure in Nepal. A Private Limited Company can have up to 101 shareholders and its shares cannot be traded publicly.

Public Limited Company

A Public Limited Company can offer shares to the public and must have at least seven shareholders.

Non-Profit Company

These companies are established for social, charitable, or community purposes rather than profit distribution.

Step-by-Step Company Registration Process

1. Company Name Reservation

The first step is reserving a unique company name through the Office of the Company Registrar online portal.

  • The name must be unique.
  • It must follow trademark regulations.
  • Restricted or illegal terms cannot be used.

Approval typically takes 1–3 working days.

2. Preparation of Required Documents

  • Memorandum of Association (MOA)
  • Articles of Association (AOA)
  • Citizenship copies of promoters
  • Passport-size photographs
  • Registered office address
  • Directors’ consent letters

3. Online Application Submission

The application is submitted through the OCR online system with details about company structure, shareholders, capital, and office location.

4. Document Verification

The Office of the Company Registrar verifies submitted documents to ensure compliance with the Companies Act.

5. Payment of Registration Fees

Authorized Capital Registration Fee
Up to NPR 100,000 NPR 1,000
NPR 100,001 – 500,000 NPR 4,500
NPR 500,001 – 2,500,000 NPR 9,500
NPR 2,500,001 – 10,000,000 NPR 16,000
Above NPR 10,000,000 NPR 19,000+

6. Certificate of Incorporation

After verification and payment, the Office of the Company Registrar issues the Certificate of Incorporation confirming the company is legally registered.

Post-Registration Requirements

  • PAN / VAT registration with Inland Revenue Department
  • Local ward office registration
  • Shareholder filing within 90 days
  • Appointment of an auditor

Estimated Registration Timeline

  • Name reservation: 1–3 days
  • Document verification: 3–5 days
  • Certificate issuance: 1 day

Total time: approximately 5–10 working days.
