VAT & Income Tax Filing in Nepal

Value Added Tax (VAT) and Income Tax filing are essential legal requirements for businesses operating in Nepal. Proper tax compliance ensures transparency in financial transactions and helps businesses avoid penalties while maintaining credibility with regulatory authorities.

Overview of Taxation in Nepal

Businesses registered in Nepal must regularly submit tax returns to the Inland Revenue Department (IRD) in accordance with the Income Tax Act 2058 and the Value Added Tax Act 2052. With the government’s digital transformation, most tax filings can now be completed through the IRD online taxpayer portal.

What is Value Added Tax (VAT)?

Value Added Tax (VAT) is a consumption tax applied to the sale of goods and services in Nepal. Businesses collect VAT from customers and submit the collected amount to the government.

The standard VAT rate in Nepal is 13%. Businesses registered for VAT must maintain proper invoices, accounting records, and submit periodic VAT returns.

What is Income Tax?

Income Tax is the tax imposed on the profits earned by individuals and businesses. Companies in Nepal must calculate their taxable income and file annual income tax returns under the Income Tax Act 2058.

The general corporate income tax rate in Nepal is 25%, although the rate may vary depending on the industry sector such as banking, insurance, and financial institutions.

VAT Registration Requirements in Nepal

Businesses must register for VAT if they meet the following criteria:

  • Annual taxable goods turnover exceeds NPR 5 million.
  • Annual taxable service turnover exceeds NPR 2 million.
  • Businesses engaged in import or export activities.
  • Manufacturing or production companies.
  • Businesses choosing voluntary VAT registration for credibility.

VAT registration is completed through the Inland Revenue Department by submitting company registration documents, PAN details, and business information.

VAT Filing Process in Nepal

1. Maintain Proper VAT Records

  • VAT invoices
  • Purchase and sales records
  • Accounting ledgers
  • Customer tax invoices

These documents are necessary for preparing VAT returns and for verification during tax audits.

2. Prepare VAT Return

A VAT return summarizes the tax collected from customers and the tax paid on business purchases.

  • Output VAT – VAT collected from sales
  • Input VAT – VAT paid on purchases

The difference between these amounts determines the VAT payable or refundable.

3. Submit VAT Return Online

VAT returns are submitted through the Inland Revenue Department’s online taxpayer portal. Businesses must log into their account and file their monthly VAT returns electronically.

4. Pay VAT Liability

If output VAT exceeds input VAT, the business must pay the remaining tax liability through authorized banks or online payment systems linked to the IRD portal.

VAT Filing Deadline

VAT returns in Nepal are generally filed monthly and must be submitted within 25 days after the end of each month.

Late submission may result in penalties, interest charges, or other compliance actions by the tax authorities.

Income Tax Filing Process

1. Prepare Financial Statements

  • Profit and Loss Statement
  • Balance Sheet
  • Cash Flow Statement

These statements are required to calculate the taxable income for the fiscal year.

2. Calculate Taxable Income

Taxable income is determined by adjusting accounting profits according to tax regulations. Some expenses are deductible while others may be disallowed under the Income Tax Act.

3. File Annual Income Tax Return

Businesses must submit their annual income tax return through the IRD online filing system along with financial statements and supporting documents.

Income Tax Filing Deadline

Income tax returns must generally be filed within three months after the end of the fiscal year.

Nepal’s fiscal year typically ends in mid-July (Shrawan) according to the Nepali calendar. Businesses may request extensions if additional time is required.

Consequences of Non-Compliance

  • Financial penalties
  • Interest on unpaid taxes
  • Tax investigations and audits
  • Legal enforcement actions

Maintaining accurate records and filing returns on time helps businesses avoid these risks.

Benefits of Proper Tax Compliance

  • Legal compliance with government regulations
  • Improved financial transparency
  • Better access to bank loans and government contracts
  • Enhanced reputation with investors and partners

Conclusion

VAT and income tax filing are critical responsibilities for businesses operating in Nepal. By maintaining proper financial records, submitting returns through the Inland Revenue Department portal, and meeting filing deadlines, companies can remain compliant with national tax regulations and operate smoothly.
